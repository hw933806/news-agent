# Aurora launches second-generation driverless trucks (Jul 22, 2026)

Aurora unveiled its **second-generation driverless trucks**, now in commercial
service across **10 routes in the Southeast U.S.** Key facts:

- **Platform shift to International LT Series.** The new fleet is built on the
  International® LT® Series and carries Aurora's next-generation autonomous
  hardware — described as more powerful and rated to last **1 million miles**.
  This realizes the International/Traton deployment the profile had pending
  "from Q2 2026."
- **Manufacturing scale-up via Roush.** Roush is installing redundant systems
  and integrating the next-gen hardware at a facility dedicated to Aurora, with
  the partners positioning Roush to reach an **annual run-rate of 1,000 Aurora
  trucks later this year** — a step-change vs the ~109-truck base and the 200+
  by end-2026 target.
- **Demand signal.** A customer's recent plan to purchase **500 Aurora
  Driver-powered trucks** underpins the route expansion (consistent with the
  Hirschbach 500-truck intent in the profile).
- **Miles.** The Aurora Driver had completed **~440,000 driverless miles** as of
  end-June 2026.
- **Independent safety validation.** Auditor Edge Case gave a positive
  three-month review of Aurora's Safety Case, backing its structure and
  alignment with major AV safety standards.

Livestream debut scheduled Jul 23, 3:30 p.m. ET. **Q2 2026 earnings: Jul 29.**

Why it matters: production scale (Roush 1,000/yr) and the OEM-integrated
second-gen kit are the gating items for the milestone thesis on this pre-revenue
name; the 500-truck customer commitment and clean third-party safety review both
de-risk the ramp.
